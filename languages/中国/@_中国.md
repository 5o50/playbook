## Key Chinese Grammar Rules for Correct Word Order

Chinese word order is generally straightforward but differs from English in several important ways. Here are the most important rules to ensure you put words in the right order:

**1. Basic Sentence Structure: Subject–Verb–Object (SVO)**
- The default sentence order in Chinese is the same as English: subject + verb + object.
  - Example: 我爱你 (Wǒ ài nǐ, "I love you")[1][2][3][4][7].

**2. Modifiers Always Precede What They Modify**
- Adjectives, adverbs, and other modifiers come before the word they describe.
  - Example: 白花 (bái huā, "white flower") — "white" comes before "flower"[3][5][10].

**3. Placement of Time Words**
- Time expressions (when something happens) usually come right after the subject or at the very beginning of the sentence.
  - Example: 我昨天去商店 (Wǒ zuótiān qù shāngdiàn, "I went to the store yesterday")[2][4][9][11].
- Both "Time + Subject + Verb + Object" and "Subject + Time + Verb + Object" are acceptable, but never put the time at the end[11].

**4. Placement of Place Words**
- Place expressions (where something happens) typically come after the time word and before the verb.
  - Example: 我在北京工作 (Wǒ zài Běijīng gōngzuò, "I work in Beijing")[10].

**5. Order of Sentence Elements**
The most common and natural order for a Chinese sentence with multiple elements is:
- Subject + Time + Manner + Place + Verb + Object
  - Example: 我昨天开心地在家用手机学中文 (Wǒ zuótiān kāixīn de zài jiā yòng shǒujī xué Zhōngwén, "I yesterday happily at home used my phone to study Chinese")[3][7].

**6. Attributives (Possessives, Descriptions)**
- Attributive phrases (including possessives) always come before the noun.
  - Example: 我的书 (wǒ de shū, "my book")[5][10].

**7. Adverbials**
- Adverbs and adverbial phrases (how, when, where, etc.) come before the verb or adjective they modify.
  - Example: 他昨天去了南京 (Tā zuótiān qùle Nánjīng, "He went to Nanjing yesterday")[5].

**8. Topic-Comment Structure**
- Chinese often uses a topic-comment structure, where the topic is stated first, followed by a comment about it. This can sometimes override strict SVO order, but the above rules still largely apply[7].

---

### Quick Reference Table

| Element  | Typical Position in Sentence   |
| -------- | ------------------------------ |
| Subject  | 1st                            |
| Time     | 2nd (or 1st, before subject)   |
| Manner   | 3rd (before verb)              |
| Place    | 4th (before verb)              |
| Verb     | After time/place/manner        |
| Object   | After verb                     |
| Modifier | Always before what it modifies |

---

**Summary of Golden Rules:**
- Use SVO order.
- Modifiers always go before the word they describe.
- Time and place come before the verb.
- Never put time or place at the end of the sentence[3][4][9][10][11].

Mastering these rules will help you form natural and correct Chinese sentences.
